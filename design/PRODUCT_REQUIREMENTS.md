# Personal Website – Product Requirements Document

## 1. Purpose

The website exists to communicate who I am as a product builder and demonstrate my ability to take products from idea to production.

It is not a traditional portfolio.

It is a professional product website that showcases my work, thinking, and execution.

---

# 2. Goals

Primary Goals

- Build credibility with founders and hiring managers.
- Showcase real product work.
- Demonstrate product thinking.
- Demonstrate engineering quality.
- Generate interview opportunities.
- Generate freelance opportunities.
- Generate remote internship opportunities.

Secondary Goals

- Build a personal brand.
- Document my journey.
- Serve as a hub for future writing.
- Showcase DuoLink.

---

# 3. Target Audience

Priority 1
- Startup Founders

Priority 2
- Product Leaders
- Hiring Managers

Priority 3
- Recruiters

Priority 4
- Engineers interested in my work.

---

# 4. Positioning

The website should communicate one message:

"I build thoughtful digital products from concept to production."

It should never feel like a resume website.

---

# 5. Brand Attributes

The website should feel:

- Premium
- Calm
- Modern
- Intentional
- Trustworthy
- Technically excellent

---

# 6. User Journey

A visitor should:

Understand what I build within 10 seconds.

Become interested in DuoLink within 30 seconds.

Understand how I think within 60 seconds.

Decide to contact me before leaving.

---

# 7. Success Metrics

The website is successful if visitors:

- View DuoLink.
- Explore my projects.
- Read about my approach.
- Contact me.
- Remember me.

---

# 8. Non-Goals

The website is NOT intended to:

- Maximize page views.
- Become a blog platform.
- Show every project I've ever built.
- Impress with flashy animations.

Quality over quantity.

---

# 9. Long-Term Vision

The website should grow alongside my career.

Eventually it should include:

- Products
- Writing
- Case Studies
- Talks
- Open Source
- Resume
- Contact
