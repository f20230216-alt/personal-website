# Personal Website – UX Specification

# 1. Experience Vision

The website should feel like the personal website of someone who builds premium digital products.

Visitors should never feel like they are browsing a student's portfolio.

Instead, they should feel they are exploring the work of someone who combines product strategy, engineering, and thoughtful execution.

The experience should be calm, premium, intentional, and memorable.

---

# 2. User Journey

## First 5 Seconds

The visitor immediately understands:

- Who I am.
- What I build.
- Why they should continue scrolling.

No confusion.

No lengthy introduction.

---

## First 30 Seconds

The visitor discovers DuoLink.

It should be obvious that this is the flagship project.

The visitor should immediately understand:

"This isn't a tutorial project."

---

## First Minute

The visitor understands:

- My approach to building products.
- My technical ability.
- My product thinking.
- My attention to quality.

---

## Final Impression

The visitor should leave believing:

"I would trust this person to build an important product."

# 4. Navigation

Minimal.

Home

Work

About

Contact

No dropdowns.

No sidebar.

No complex navigation.

---

# 5. Scrolling Philosophy

Every section should naturally lead into the next.

Scrolling should feel effortless.

Large amounts of whitespace.

No visual clutter.

No abrupt transitions.

---

# 6. Mobile Experience

Mobile is first-class.

Not a responsive afterthought.

Every section should be designed for:

Desktop

Tablet

Mobile

---

# 7. Interaction Philosophy

Every interaction should feel intentional.

Hover effects should communicate affordance.

Animations should reinforce hierarchy.

Never animate for decoration.

---

# 8. Motion Philosophy

Motion should feel:

Calm

Premium

Responsive

Natural

Never distracting.

No exaggerated animations.

No bouncing.

No spinning.

No unnecessary parallax.

---

# 9. Accessibility

Keyboard accessible.

Readable typography.

High contrast.

Semantic HTML.

Reduced motion support.

Screen-reader friendly.

---

# 10. Performance Goals

Instant first impression.

Fast page transitions.

Images optimized.

Minimal JavaScript.

Excellent Lighthouse score.

No layout shifts.

Responsive interactions.

---

# 11. Emotional Goals

The visitor should feel:

Confidence.

Trust.

Curiosity.

Professionalism.

Craftsmanship.

---

# 12. Primary CTA

"Let's build something meaningful together."

The CTA should appear naturally after the visitor has explored the website.

Never immediately upon arrival.

---

# 13. UX Principles

Show, don't tell.

Evidence over claims.

Depth over quantity.

Premium over flashy.

Clarity over cleverness.

Craft over complexity.

Products before resume.

Always optimize for trust.
