# Personal Website – Content Strategy

# 1. Content Philosophy

The website should communicate credibility through evidence rather than claims.

Do not say:

"I am passionate."

Show products.

Do not say:

"I am a quick learner."

Show growth.

Do not say:

"I am a great leader."

Show leadership experiences.

Every section should answer a question a founder naturally asks.

---

# 2. Core Story

The website tells one story.

"I enjoy solving meaningful problems by combining product thinking, engineering, and execution."

Everything should reinforce this.

---

# 3. Hero

Purpose

Capture attention immediately.

Answer:

Who am I?

What do I build?

Why should you keep scrolling?

Avoid long introductions.

---

# 4. Currently Building

Purpose

Show active work.

Current focus:

DuoLink

Display:

Project

Current status

Technology

Short summary

Link to case study (future)

---

# 5. Featured Work

Purpose

Demonstrate execution.

Each project should include:

Problem

Approach

Outcome

Lessons

Technology

Never describe projects only by technology.

---

# 6. Product Thinking

Purpose

Show how I think.

Potential topics:

Architecture

Product decisions

UX tradeoffs

Scaling decisions

Research

This section is more important than listing technologies.

---

# 7. Experience

Purpose

Show responsibility.

Focus on:

Ownership

Leadership

Execution

Impact

Not job descriptions.

---

# 8. Skills

Purpose

Support the projects.

Skills should never become the main attraction.

Projects come first.

---

# 9. Contact

Purpose

Start conversations.

Not simply collect email addresses.

The visitor should feel invited to build something together.

---

# 10. Future Writing

Future articles may include:

Building DuoLink

Architecture

Flutter

Product Strategy

Career Lessons

Founder Notes

Engineering Decisions

---

# 11. Tone

Professional.

Warm.

Confident.

Thoughtful.

Never arrogant.

Never overly casual.

Never corporate.

---

# 12. Writing Principles

Use short paragraphs.

Avoid buzzwords.

Prefer evidence.

Avoid repetition.

Avoid unnecessary adjectives.

Every sentence should add value.

---

# 13. Future Expansion

Future sections may include:

Case Studies

Open Source

Talks

Reading List

Recommendations

Without changing the site's core structure.

---

# 14. Success Criteria

A visitor should leave believing:

This person can take an idea and turn it into a high-quality product.

That is the single most important message on the website.
