# Personal Website – Moodboard

# Overall Feeling

The website should feel like the workspace of someone who builds exceptional digital products.

Visitors should immediately associate the experience with:

- Craftsmanship
- Precision
- Clarity
- Confidence
- Premium quality

---

# Visual Keywords

- Minimal
- Editorial
- Elegant
- Intentional
- Warm
- Quiet
- Modern
- Sophisticated
- Timeless

---

# Typography References

Large editorial headlines.

Generous line spacing.

Readable body copy.

High contrast between headline and body.

Typography should communicate confidence before color does.

---

# Layout References

Large whitespace.

Strong visual rhythm.

Asymmetrical balance.

Full-width hero.

Comfortable reading width.

Alternating visual density.

---

# Color Direction

Primary

Deep charcoal backgrounds.

Secondary

Warm neutral surfaces.

Accent

One restrained accent color used sparingly.

Color should never become the focal point.

---

# Lighting

Soft.

Ambient.

Subtle.

No dramatic glows.

No harsh gradients.

No heavy shadows.

---

# Imagery

Real product screenshots.

Device mockups.

Minimal diagrams.

No stock photos.

No AI art.

No decorative illustrations.

---

# Components

Simple.

Large.

Comfortable.

Rounded corners.

Soft borders.

Minimal shadows.

---

# Motion

Calm.

Natural.

Purposeful.

Smooth.

Motion should communicate hierarchy instead of decoration.

---

# Scroll Experience

The website should feel like reading a premium digital magazine.

Each section naturally introduces the next.

Scrolling should feel effortless.

---

# Inspiration Sources

The goal is to achieve the level of craftsmanship demonstrated by products such as:

- Apple
- Linear
- Vercel
- Notion
- Raycast
- Stripe

These are references for quality, not layouts.

Never imitate them directly.

---

# What To Avoid

- Neon colors
- Glassmorphism everywhere
- Heavy gradients
- Excessive animations
- Busy backgrounds
- Oversized icons
- Corporate layouts
- Dashboard aesthetics
- Template-looking portfolios
- Generic developer portfolio designs

---

# Success Criteria

Someone visiting the website should immediately think:

"This person builds premium products."

The website should communicate quality before the visitor reads a single paragraph.
